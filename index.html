Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan

Here are details about Nusrat:

1948: Khan was born in Faisalabad, Punjab, Pakistan. His ancestors learned music and singing and adopted it as a profession.
His father, Fateh Ali Khan, was a musicologist, vocalist, instrumentalist and a qawwal (related to Qawwali, a form of Sufi Islamic devotional singing).

1964: Death of Khan's father. The musical education of Khan was then under led under the supervision of his paternal uncles. By this time, his family already recognized him as a talented musician and singer.

1971: After the death of his uncle, Khan became the official leader of the family Qawwali party. Khan's first public performance as the leader of this party, was at a studio recording.

1985: In the summero of this year, Khan performed at the World of Music, Arts and Dance festival in London.

1987: He performed at the 5th Asian Traditional Performing Art Festival in Japan.

1988: Khan teamed up with Peter Gabriel on the soundtrack of The Last Temptation of Christ.

1997: Khan's album Intoxicated Spirit was nominated for a Grammy Award for Best Traditional Folk Album.

1997: He died of a sudden cardiac arrest at Cromwell Hospital on August 16th, at the age of 48.

Awards and influence

Khan is widely considered to be the most important qawwal in history. Many honorary titles were bestowed upon Khan during his 25-year music career. In 2005, he posthumously recieved the "Legends" award at the UK Asian Music Awards. Khan is often credited as one of the creators of "world music"
He recorded hundreds of albums around the glon. OSA, Birmingham released about 125 albums and 30 to 35 concert films. He recorded over 40 cassettes in Pakistan, many of which are available under the EMI label.
